Marie Maynard Daly

Courage is like-- it's a habitus, a habit, a virtue: you get it by courageous acts. It's like you learn to swim by swimming. You learn courage by couraging.

Dr. Daly, born April 16, 1921, was the first Black American woman in the United States to earn a Ph.D in Chemistry. She attended Columbia University. Dr. Daly discovered the link between high cholesterol and clogged arteries. She also studied the impact of sugar on arteries, and how cigarette smoke effected lungs. She passed away October 28, 2003.

Interesting Facts

  1. Started a Queens College scholarship fund in her father's honor to assist minority students majoring in chemistry or physics.
  2. Served as an investigator for the American Heart Association.
  3. Taught biochemistry and science at Albert Einstein College of Medicine at Yeshiva University.
  4. In 2016, PS 360Q in Queens, NY was named Dr. Marie M. Daly Academy of Excellence.
